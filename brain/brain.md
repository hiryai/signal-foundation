# Brain

This file is the consolidated Brain layer. It has five sections — Expertise, Audience, Voice, Offers, Beliefs — and is populated by the `/setup-brain` slash command, not by hand.

Each section below is unfilled. An HTML-comment sentinel at the end of each section marks it as not-yet-populated. When you run `/setup-brain`, the command detects each sentinel, asks you the relevant questions, and replaces the structural prompts plus the sentinel with your actual answer.

---

## Expertise

What you know deeply enough to teach without notes.

- The domain or domains you have real depth in
- The specific problems inside that domain you have solved repeatedly
- The unfair advantages, frameworks, or shortcuts you have developed
- The level of sophistication of the audience you can credibly serve

## Audience

Who you are talking to.

- Who they are at a job-title or life-stage level
- What outcome they want from you
- What they have already tried that did not work
- What they know and do not know about your domain
- Where they hang out and how they describe their own problem in their own words

<!-- fill -->

## Voice

How you sound on the page and on camera.

- The tone register you use (plain, technical, irreverent, formal, etc.)
- Words and phrases you use often
- Words and phrases you refuse to use
- Sentence length and rhythm preferences
- The stance you take toward the audience (peer, coach, operator, teacher, etc.)

<!-- fill -->

## Offers

What you sell.

- Each offer's name
- Each offer's promise (the outcome the buyer is paying for)
- Each offer's delivery mechanism (course, cohort, software, service, community, etc.)
- Who each offer is for and who it is not for
- The price tier of each offer described qualitatively (entry, mid, premium) without specific numbers

<!-- fill -->

## Beliefs

The strong opinions that make you distinctive.

- Things most people in your space believe that you think are wrong
- Principles you defend even when they cost you customers
- The worldview your work is built on
- The trade-offs you accept that others refuse to accept
